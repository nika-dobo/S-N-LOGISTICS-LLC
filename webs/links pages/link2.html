Container Specifications

Global Shipping Container Types

Standardizing shipping containers revolutionized global trade. Understanding the exact dimensions and weight capacities of standard containers is essential for maximizing space and ensuring safe transit of your cargo.

20' Standard Container (20ft Dry)

The standard 20-foot container is the most common unit for general purpose cargo. It typically carries heavy commodities like machinery, metal, or dense consumer goods.
Internal dimensions: 5.9m (L) x 2.35m (W) x 2.39m (H)
Capacity: ~33.2 cubic meters
Max Payload: ~28,200 kg

40' Standard Container (40ft Dry)

Ideal for voluminous but lighter cargo. Used heavily in the textile, electronics, and consumer retail industries.
Internal dimensions: 12.03m (L) x 2.35m (W) x 2.39m (H)
Capacity: ~67.7 cubic meters
Max Payload: ~26,600 kg

40' High Cube (40HC)

Offers an extra foot of height compared to the standard 40ft, making it perfect for light, bulky cargo or overheight industrial equipment.
Internal dimensions: 12.03m (L) x 2.35m (W) x 2.69m (H)
Capacity: ~76.3 cubic meters
Max Payload: ~26,580 kg

"Properly matching your cargo volume and density to the correct container guarantees optimal freight rates and zero transit delays."
